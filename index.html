Duration: 30 Minutes

Description:

Waxing is a hair removal technique that occurs when warm to hot wax is applied to the skin in the direction of the hair growth. Before the wax cools down a muslin cloth strip is applied to the wax with pressure. To remove the wax which contains the hair, the skin is hold tot and the strip is pulled in the opposite direction. After all hair has been removed, a wax remover oil is applied to the skin.
Azulene Wax and All-Purpose Honey Wax are both offered.
It is best to get waxing done when the hair growth is about a quarter of an inch to half an inch in length. Anything shorter won’t pull out properly and anything longer will cause pain and discomfort. After waxing is it best to avoid any exfoliation or pedicures that include leg massages in the next 24 hours.

Bleaching

Duration: 30 Minutes

Description:

Bleaching is the best way to lighten hair when someone doesn’t want to remove any of it. Usually it is a cream that is mixed before use. It’s applied where the hair needs to be lightened. It’s left on for no more than 8-10 minutes and then rinsed off.
Before bleaching, it is recommended to do a patch test on the inner elbow of any client intending on getting this service done at least 24 hours ahead of time. The purpose of a patch test is to insure that the client doesn’t react badly to the product.
The type of bleach I use is Cinol Gold Bleach.

Hair Color
  • Accent Foils: $5 each
  • Partial Highlights: $15
  • Half Head Highlights: $20
  • Full Head Highlights: $30
  • Balayage Highlights: $35
  • Full Head Applications: $32
  • Retouch: $15

Duration: 30 Minutes

Description:

Hair dye is applied when a different hair color is desired, whether it’s lightening or darkening. Each application is different and requires more or less products and effort. The prices are listed accordingly.
The hair dye is mixed with a developer which is usually made of ammonia. The mixture is applied to the hair strands and begins to oxidize. The first 10-15 minutes of being on the strand is when the mixture is opening up the cuticle and exposing the cortex layer. This layer of the hair contains hair pigmentation. The remaining time the dye sits in the hair is when the depositing of the color happens.
When applying color, it isn’t damaging to leave it on for too long accidentally. On the other hand if bleach, which is used to lighten hair, is left on for too long it can cause a lighter shade which isn’t desired or worse; the hair frying and falling off.
With that being said, bleach doesn’t come in a liquid form. The enlightener is a powder that is mixed with developer as well. The process of bleaching is typically similar to hair dye except it requires to have a eye kept on it at all times. When mixed and applied to the strands it opens up the cortex and eats away the hair pigmentation, thus lightening it.
Each procedure is followed by shampoo and blowout. It is recommended to have a hair treatment done after all color services. This is due to chemicals being harsh on the hair strands and stripping the natural oils they need to stay strong.

Henna / Mehndhi
  • Small Design (Name, word, or small pattern): $5
  • $42 an hour

Duration: 30 Minutes

Description:

Mendhi, or more commonly known as Henna, is a traditional paste that’s been passed down many generations for years. The paste contains henna leaves that have been ground up into powder and mixed with water, sometimes even oils. The paste is applied to the desired area and left on for as long as possible. It starts to stain the skin as soon as applied and as it becomes dry, it flakes off naturally. If a darker color is desired it is best to dampen the henna with sugar, lemon water. Not a full pitcher of lemonade, but a small amount with a cotton ball. After the henna has flaked off or has been removed with baby powder it is recommended to avoid water f or a deeper stain. Applying an oil to the design after removing is optional for better color. The first day the stain will be a bright orange, the second day the stain will be a red or light brown, and the third day is when the stain will be the darkest; deep red or brown. Factors that play into the color of the mendhi are but aren’t limited to:

  • Warmth of the area applied
  • Temperature
  • Amount of time kept on
  • Contact with water
